3PL LOGISTICS SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Agreement is entered into between Northstar Retail Pvt. Ltd. ("Client") and
Velocity Freight & Warehousing Solutions ("3PL Provider"), effective from
1st April 2025.

================================================================================
SECTION 1 — DEFINITIONS
================================================================================

1.1 "OTIF" means On-Time-In-Full, defined as the percentage of orders delivered
on or before the scheduled delivery date, with no shortages or discrepancies
in quantity, as measured against the Purchase Order.

1.2 "SLA" means Service Level Agreement, the performance thresholds defined in
Section 3 of this Agreement.

1.3 "Cold Chain SKU" means any stock-keeping unit that requires storage and
transportation within a temperature range of 2°C to 8°C at all times.

1.4 "Force Majeure Event" means any event beyond the reasonable control of
either party, including but not limited to natural disasters, government
lockdowns, strikes exceeding 5 consecutive days, and pandemics officially
declared by the World Health Organization.

================================================================================
SECTION 2 — SCOPE OF SERVICES
================================================================================

2.1 The 3PL Provider shall provide warehousing, inventory management,
order fulfilment, and last-mile delivery services across the following
zones: North India, West India, and South India.

2.2 The 3PL Provider shall maintain a minimum of 4 operational warehouses
with a combined storage capacity of no less than 250,000 square feet.

2.3 Cold Chain SKUs shall be stored exclusively in temperature-controlled
zones equipped with continuous monitoring and automated alerting systems.

================================================================================
SECTION 3 — SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENT (SLA)
================================================================================

3.1 OTIF PENALTY CLAUSE

The 3PL Provider guarantees a minimum OTIF performance of 95% measured on a
monthly basis. For each percentage point below 95% OTIF in a given month, the
Client shall be entitled to a penalty deduction equal to 2% of that month's
total invoice value. The maximum penalty deduction in any single month shall
not exceed 20% of that month's total invoice value.

Example: If OTIF performance for a month is 91% (4 percentage points below
the 95% threshold), the penalty shall be 4 x 2% = 8% of that month's invoice
value.

3.2 PRIORITY ORDER FULFILMENT WINDOW

Orders flagged as "Priority" by the Client must be picked, packed, and
dispatched within 4 hours of order receipt during operational hours
(8:00 AM to 8:00 PM, Monday to Saturday). Orders received outside operational
hours shall be processed within 4 hours of the next operational window.

3.3 STANDARD ORDER FULFILMENT WINDOW

Non-priority orders must be picked, packed, and dispatched within 24 hours
of order receipt.

3.4 INVENTORY ACCURACY

The 3PL Provider shall maintain a minimum inventory accuracy of 98%, verified
through monthly cycle counts. Inventory accuracy below 98% in any given month
shall result in a corrective action plan being submitted by the 3PL Provider
within 5 business days.

================================================================================
SECTION 4 — COLD CHAIN REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

4.1 MINIMUM ORDER QUANTITY (COLD CHAIN)

For Cold Chain SKUs, the minimum order quantity (MOQ) per dispatch shall be
50 units per SKU. Orders below this threshold may be consolidated with other
Cold Chain orders to the same delivery zone, subject to the Priority Order
Fulfilment Window defined in Section 3.2.

4.2 TEMPERATURE EXCURSION REPORTING

Any temperature excursion outside the 2°C to 8°C range lasting longer than
30 minutes must be reported to the Client within 2 hours of detection, along
with the affected SKUs and batch numbers.

================================================================================
SECTION 5 — RETURNS AND RMA PROCESS
================================================================================

5.1 RETURN MERCHANDISE AUTHORISATION (RMA) — DAMAGED GOODS

For goods damaged in transit or storage, the following RMA process applies:

  Step 1: The receiving warehouse shall photograph the damaged goods and log
          the damage in the Warehouse Management System (WMS) within 24 hours
          of receipt.

  Step 2: A Damage Report, including photographs, SKU details, and quantity,
          shall be generated and shared with the Client within 48 hours.

  Step 3: The Client shall review the Damage Report and issue an RMA
          disposition (return to vendor, write-off, or liquidate) within
          5 business days.

  Step 4: Upon receiving the disposition, the 3PL Provider shall execute the
          instruction within 3 business days and update the WMS accordingly.

5.2 RMA processing fees are included in the standard service fee and shall
not be billed separately.

================================================================================
SECTION 6 — RATE STRUCTURE AND COMMITMENTS
================================================================================

6.1 The Client commits to a minimum monthly order volume of 15,000 orders
across all zones combined.

6.2 Should the Client's actual order volume fall below 12,000 orders in any
given month, a shortfall fee equal to the per-order rate multiplied by the
difference between 12,000 and the actual volume shall apply.

6.3 Rate escalation: Service rates shall be revised annually on the
anniversary of the Effective Date, capped at a maximum increase of 6% per
annum, based on mutually agreed inflation indices.

================================================================================
SECTION 7 — FORCE MAJEURE
================================================================================

7.1 Neither party shall be liable for delays or failures in performance
resulting from a Force Majeure Event, provided that the affected party
notifies the other party in writing within 48 hours of the event's onset.

7.2 If a Force Majeure Event persists for more than 30 consecutive days,
either party may terminate this Agreement with 15 days' written notice,
without penalty.

================================================================================
SECTION 8 — TERM AND TERMINATION
================================================================================

8.1 This Agreement shall remain in effect for an initial term of 24 months
from the Effective Date, automatically renewing for successive 12-month
periods unless either party provides 90 days' written notice of
non-renewal.

8.2 Either party may terminate this Agreement for material breach, provided
the breaching party is given 30 days to cure the breach following written
notice.
